Phase 2 · Audit

The Condenser doesn't feel like a BuildCore product yet.

The existing kit uses the design system's tokens (warm bone paper, ink, the orange + status palette, Archivo+Geist+Mono) but it doesn't announce the BuildCore brand — no wedge mark, no parent-product framing, and the language still says "Lot 247" everywhere. Below: what's already working, what's drifting, and what to change first.

Keep

Token discipline is real

All screens use colors_and_type.css. Stamp shadow + heavy borders + warm paper are correctly applied. This is the foundation we'll build on.

Keep

Information architecture is sound

5 screens (Today / Sites / Punch list / Detail / New) match a real CM's mental model. We're not changing the bones — just the surface and the home-screen hierarchy.

Fix · brand

No visible BuildCore connection

Nothing on the home screen says "this is the field arm of BuildCore." After Clock In, the user should land somewhere that feels like the same company. Every direction below adds the wedge + wordmark in the header.

Fix · copy

"Lot" is everywhere

"Lot 247", "Lots in walk-down", "Lots" tab — locked rule: addresses only. All three directions below rename to Sites in nav and address-only ("14 Sycamore Ln") in the body.

Fix · hierarchy

The home is a stat dashboard, not a starting point

The current Today screen leads with three small stat tiles. For a CM about to walk into a house, the question is "what do I do next" — not "what are my totals." Directions B and C reframe around that.

Fix · CTA

Primary action is generic

"Add punch" is technically correct but uninspiring. We should consider "Start the walk" / "Walk it now" — verbs tied to the activity, not the database operation. Each direction tries a different CTA.

Phase 3 · Direction options

Three takes on the home screen.

Drag artboards to reorder, double-click any title to rename, click the focus icon to expand one fullscreen. All three use the BuildCore tokens — what differs is hierarchy, density, and what the screen tells you to do next.
